This is my final project for the Olin First-Year class called Modeling and Simulation of the Physical World. My partner and I chose to simulate a game of skee ball which includes human error. Our main question was, which ring should you aim for to maximize your points? You would think the answer would be the 100 ring, but our model showed that aiming for the 40 ring actually earned the highest scored on average, considering human error. I worked in a group of three to make an Electromyogram for the Olin First-Year class Introduction to Sensors, Instrumentation, and Measurement. An electromyogram (EMG) is a device that measures electrical muscle activity. Our EMG consisted of a circuit to amplify and filter the signal from three electrodes placed on the skin.
